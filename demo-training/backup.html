1. Zimbra OpenPGP Training


    Some practical exercises to become familiar with Zimbra OpenPGP.

  1. Exercise 1 - Decrypt and verify an email message

    1. Download training-zimbra-openpgp.tgz to your computer.
    2. Log on to your Zimbra account.
    3. Import the training-zimbra-openpgp.tgz file to your account.
      preferences -> import
    4. Go to the newly imported folder in your mailbox called 'training-zimbra-openpgp'.
    5. View the email with subject 'Demo key pair'.
    6. Configure the Zimbra OpenPGP Zimlet for use with the private key, public key and passphrase from to the 'Demo key pair' email.
    7. Decrypt and verify the email with subject 'Test message'

    This should throw an error... but you know what to do!

  1. Exercise 2 - generate a keypair

    Generate a keypair for use with Zimbra OpenPGP Zimlet, make sure to store your results in a safe place.

    If you already are a PGP user, configure Zimbra OpenPGP Zimlet to use your existing private key, public key and passphrase

  1.  Exercise 3 - Encrypt an email message

    1. Go to https://pgp.mit.edu and find the public key for b.de.graaff
    2. Add b.de.graaff as a trusted public key in Zimbra OpenPGP Zimlet 
    3. Share your public key (from exercise 2) with b.de.graaff via an encrypted plain-text email message, make sure to use the key with fingerprint: 86dbe15de4f5bb1a197bfa39c80b94e79422863a
    4. Ask the recipient if your mail could be decrypted

  1. Exercise 4 - Decrypt a message
    using your private key

    1. Decrypt the email message that has been send to you by b.de.graaff (using the results of exercise 2 and 3)
